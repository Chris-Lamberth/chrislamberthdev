Safety + LGBTQ+ travel note

Costa Rica is one of the stronger Central America options for an LGBTQ-inclusive group trip. Same-sex marriage has been legal in Costa Rica since 2020, and the country is generally considered one of the more LGBTQ-friendly destinations in the region.

Common-sense planning: We’ll use private airport transportation, vetted tour companies, group travel when possible, well-reviewed rentals, and a WhatsApp group chat for coordination. The current U.S. State Department advisory is Level 2: Exercise Increased Caution, mainly due to general crime risks such as theft and some violent crime affecting tourists.

Weather snapshot

Our dates are right at the transition into dry season. Costa Rica has microclimates, so weather varies by region.

Manuel Antonio

Warm, humid, tropical, and lush. Expect beach/pool weather with possible quick tropical showers. Great for national park, catamaran, rafting, waterfalls, beach and pool time.

La Fortuna / Arenal

Warm rainforest weather. It can be wetter and cloudier than the coast, and volcano views depend on cloud cover. Great for hot springs, waterfalls, hanging bridges and rainforest activities.
