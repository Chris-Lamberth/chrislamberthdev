🎭 Choose Your Nighttime Chaos

Option A • theatrical chaos

The Dinner Detective — Murder Mystery Dinner

The vibe: interactive, suspicious, dramatic, and hilarious.

We get dinner while a “crime” unfolds around us, and the actors are hidden among the guests. This means one of us could absolutely be accused of murder for entertainment purposes.

Estimate: ~$75/person

Option B • ridiculous history

Savannah for Morons — Comedy Trolley Tour

The vibe: campy, fast, goofy, and intentionally ridiculous.

A 90-minute comedy ride that roasts Savannah history with costumes, dumb jokes, and high-energy storytelling. Basically: educational, but in a very unserious way.

Estimate: ~$59/person

Option C • spooky photo ops

Hearse Ghost Tours

The vibe: spooky, campy, weird, and very Friday-the-13th appropriate.

We ride through the city in a converted hearse while hearing haunted stories, dark local lore, and macabre Savannah tales. Peak sibling-photo material.

Estimate: ~$25/person

👾 Sunday: Weird & Wonderful Grand Finale

oddities & pinball

Graveface Museum + Graveface Records

An atmospheric true-crime and oddities museum packed with cult curiosities, cryptids, taxidermy, dark history, and horror-themed pinball included with admission.

Estimate: ~$20/person

boat ride cooldown

Savannah Riverboat Sightseeing Cruise

A breezy paddlewheel cruise along the Savannah River so we can unwind, look at the skyline, and feel like spooky Southern socialites before heading home.

Estimate: ~$42/person

🌤️ Weather + Packing Notes

  • November is usually a great time to visit: mild days, cooler evenings, and fewer crowds.
  • Bring layers for night tours and the riverboat.
  • Wear comfortable shoes — Savannah means walking, uneven sidewalks, and cobblestones.
  • Tybee can feel windier than downtown, so a light jacket is smart.

🧭 Safety + Practical Notes

  • Stay together downtown at night, especially after tours or drinks.
  • Use rideshare if parking is annoying or if anyone has been drinking.
  • Check Tybee parking rules and meters before leaving the car.
  • Fort Pulaski entrance is cashless, so bring a card.
